Selling Strategy

Method of Sale

Auction

Auction is the most powerful method for maximising sale price in a competitive market. It creates a transparent, emotionally-charged environment where multiple buyers compete openly — removing the ceiling on price and placing all negotiating power firmly in your hands. On auction day, the hammer falls on an unconditional contract, giving you absolute certainty of sale without delay.

For a property like yours on Magill Road, with broad buyer appeal across owner-occupiers, upgraders, and investors, auction is the ideal method. It compresses urgency, forces decisions, and consistently outperforms private sale in competitive inner-east markets. Willow's structured lead-up — including intensive buyer follow-up in the final two weeks and a collaborative reserve price meeting — ensures you are positioned for the strongest possible result on the day.

  • Unconditional sale at the fall of the hammer — no finance or B&P clauses
  • No ceiling on price — buyers determine value through open competition
  • Vendor controls the reserve — you accept nothing below your minimum
  • Strong buyer urgency created by the fixed auction date
  • Willow conducts a full reserve consultation in the week prior to auction
  • Post-auction negotiation available if property passes in

Private Sale

A private sale (private treaty) allows buyers to submit offers at any time throughout the campaign. There is no fixed deadline — the campaign runs until the right offer is received and accepted. This method suits vendors who prefer a less pressured timeline, or properties where the buyer pool is more selective and may require additional time to arrange finance or conduct due diligence.

Negotiation under private sale is managed confidentially by your agent. We present all offers in writing, walk you through every term, and provide professional guidance on how to respond — whether that is acceptance, counter-offer, or rejection. You remain in complete control at every step.

  • Flexible duration — campaign continues until the right offer arrives
  • Buyers may include finance and building inspection conditions
  • Cooling-off period of 2 business days applies in South Australia
  • Negotiation managed privately — buyers do not know each other's position
  • Well-suited to unique properties or slower-paced market segments
  • Vendor can accept, reject, or counter any offer received

Best Offers by Date

Best Offers by Date merges the urgency of a deadline with the flexibility of private treaty. All interested buyers are asked to submit their best and final offer by a specific date and time — creating genuine competition without the formality or emotion of a public auction. This method works particularly well where multiple buyers are interested but one or more may prefer to avoid auction pressure.

Once the deadline passes, we present all offers to you simultaneously. You review each offer on its merits — price, deposit, conditions, and settlement terms — and choose the outcome that best serves your goals. You are under no obligation to accept any offer received.

  • Fixed deadline creates urgency and prompts buyers to act
  • All offers received simultaneously — prevents progressive counter-bidding
  • Conditions such as finance and B&P may be included by buyers
  • Vendor selects the best overall offer, not just the highest price
  • Less confrontational atmosphere — suits vendors preferring a quieter process
  • Can be combined with private sale if the deadline produces no acceptable offers

Expression of Interest (EOI)

An Expression of Interest campaign invites serious buyers to submit a written proposal detailing their offered price, conditions, deposit amount, and preferred settlement date. EOI is most appropriate for prestige properties, development sites, or properties with a highly specific buyer profile that requires more time and deliberation before committing.

The EOI process is more formal and deliberate than a standard private sale. Interested parties are given a set period — typically four to six weeks — to conduct their due diligence before submitting a sealed bid. This filters out casual enquiry and attracts only the most motivated, well-prepared buyers.

  • Suited to premium, prestige, or complex properties requiring deep buyer research
  • Sealed bids ensure buyer confidentiality and encourage genuine best offers
  • Allows bespoke settlement dates and flexible terms tailored to your needs
  • Vendor has complete discretion — no obligation to accept any offer
  • Can accommodate multiple rounds of negotiation with shortlisted buyers
  • Typically run over a longer 4–6 week campaign period

Our Process

What to Expect

Open House Inspections

We conduct professionally managed open inspections, typically on Saturdays and Wednesdays throughout the campaign. Every open inspection is personally attended by Mohit — not a junior agent — so every buyer is greeted with authority, expertise, and genuine relationship-building intent.

Prior to each inspection, we provide a preparation checklist and conduct a pre-inspection walkthrough to ensure your home is staged, lit, and presented in its absolute best light. First impressions shape price expectations, and we take this seriously.

After every inspection, you will receive a written feedback summary: number of groups through, interest levels, any specific buyer comments, and our recommended next steps. Private inspections are arranged promptly for serious buyers who cannot attend scheduled opens — no opportunity is ever left unpursued.

Buyer Management

Every enquiry — whether by phone, email, or portal — is followed up within 24 hours. We qualify buyers early, understanding their motivation, financial capacity, and decision timeline. This knowledge is invaluable at negotiation time, when knowing who is truly committed makes all the difference.

From the moment your property is listed, we match it against our active buyer database and contact all relevant prospects directly. This pre-market exposure frequently generates genuine interest and private inspection requests before the first open house has even taken place — a significant competitive advantage.

Throughout the campaign we maintain a structured buyer register, tracking every enquiry, inspection attendance, and follow-up conversation. You will always know who is engaged, how seriously, and what it will take to convert them into a sale.

Offers & Contracts

All offers are submitted in writing and presented to you promptly and in full. We walk you through every term — offered price, deposit amount, conditions (if any), settlement date, and any special conditions — ensuring you fully understand your options before making any decision.

We never pressure you into accepting an offer that does not meet your expectations. Our role is to present all facts clearly, give you our professional recommendation, and act exclusively in your best interests. If an offer falls short, we tell you exactly why — and what strategy we recommend to improve it.

Where multiple offers are received, we manage the process strategically to maximise competition and outcome. Once an offer is accepted, we coordinate with your solicitor to ensure contracts are prepared, executed, and exchanged promptly so the deal doesn't fall over on a technicality.

Auction Lead-Up

The final two weeks before auction are the most critical phase of the campaign. We shift into high-intensity buyer management mode — personally calling every registered and prospective bidder to gauge commitment, resolve any remaining concerns, and confirm their intent to attend and bid.

In the week prior to auction, we schedule a dedicated vendor meeting to discuss your reserve price. This conversation is grounded in real buyer feedback, current market comparisons, and a clear view of who we expect to bid and at what level. You will never walk into auction day uninformed or unprepared.

We also prepare you for the auction experience itself: what to expect from the auctioneer, how bidding typically progresses, when and how the reserve is announced, and what happens if the property passes in. Confidence on the day comes from preparation in the days before.

Negotiation

Skilled negotiation is where great agents earn their fee — and where average agents lose their clients thousands. Our approach is principled, strategic, and always disciplined. We never reveal your position, never show desperation, and never negotiate against ourselves.

We create competition wherever it exists, leverage genuine interest without alienating buyers, and use every legitimate tool available — timing, transparency, silence, and counter-strategy — to maximise your result. Every negotiation is different, and we adapt our approach to the specific dynamics of each buyer and each situation.

If the property passes in at auction, we immediately transition into direct negotiation with the highest bidder, using the energy and urgency of auction day to our advantage. Our goal is always the best possible price, on the best possible terms, in the shortest possible time.
